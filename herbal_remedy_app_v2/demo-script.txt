BEFORE YOU BEGIN
Three rules. Non-negotiable.
Never explain how it works. The magic dies the moment you mention a graph database.
Never rush the pauses. The silence is the demo.
Never fake the data. Every word on screen must come from your real conversation history.
Total runtime: 6 minutes. Not one second more.

Your 6-month conversation history is ingested.

All five demo queries are saved — ready to paste, not type.
Model switcher is set to Claude.
Phone on silent. Notifications off. Do not disturb on.
Video recording running as backup.

IF THEY ASK HOW IT WORKS
You have one answer. Use it every time.
"It works the way human memory works. It understands what matters, remembers what is current, and forgets what you let go. The technology is not interesting. What it does for you is."
Then redirect:
"What would it mean for your work if your AI actually knew you?"
That question is worth more than any technical explanation.
IF SOMETHING BREAKS
Say this:
"This is a four-week prototype running on my laptop. The architecture works. What I am showing you is the proof of concept, not the product. Let me show you the recorded version."
Then play the pre-recorded video. You always have a pre-recorded video.
REHEARSE THIS 20 TIMES BEFORE YOU DO IT ONCE.
The demo is not the product. The demo is the story.
Tell it like you mean it. Because you do.
